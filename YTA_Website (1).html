Our Story

About Youth Thalassemic Alliance

Founded in 2011 with a dream, a purpose, and a promise to Payal — that thalassemia would no longer stand in the way of a fulfilling life.

Our Origin

A Dream Turned Into a Movement

Youth Thalassemic Alliance (Y.T.A) was formed in the memory of Miss Payal Wadhawa, our dear friend and sister of Miss Sangeeta Wadhawa, Founder of Y.T.A, in February 2011.

"She always believed that everyone is equal and each one of us should get the equal opportunity to grow, prove their capability, face the world with up-coming challenges, and also give back to the society as a responsible citizen."

Y.T.A was formed to bring Payal's dream into reality — a world where thalassemic patients are empowered, supported, and never alone.

Our Motto

  • Assist thalassaemic patients in securing medical treatment, hospitalization, and blood transfusions.
  • Monitor closely the medication, growth and thalassemia management for registered patients.
  • Conduct blood donation camps in general and for thalassaemia patients in particular.
  • Organize and assist in screening and medical camps for thalassaemia patients.
  • Provide regular counseling and guidance centers for thalassaemia patients.
  • Organize social events and build community for thalassaemia patients.
  • Create awareness through lectures, pamphlets, books, and electronic media.

Our Work

Projects & Activities

From blood donation drives to international webinars — here's how Y.T.A transforms lives every single day.

Current Projects
What We Run
🩸

Blood Donation Camps

Regular blood donation drives organized in partnership with local hospitals and voluntary organizations. Special camps are dedicated to ensuring thalassemic patients always have access to safe, compatible blood across India.

Ongoing · Community
🏥

Medical Screening Camps

Organized medical camps for detecting thalassemia carriers early. Our camps focus on high-risk populations including prospective couples and pregnant women to prevent thalassemic births through timely intervention.

Periodic · Medical
💬

Counseling & Guidance Centre

Regular counseling sessions for patients, families, and carriers. We offer emotional support, practical guidance, and resources to help navigate life with thalassemia — empowering patients to live with dignity and confidence.

Regular · Welfare
🎉

Social Events & Community

Organizing social gatherings, festivals, and cultural events to build a strong, connected community among thalassemic patients — because belonging, friendship, and joy matter as much as medicine.

Periodic · Social
📚

Awareness & Education

Publishing pamphlets, books, and conducting lectures to spread awareness about thalassemia across schools, colleges, and the wider public through electronic, print, and digital media platforms.

Continuous · Awareness
🎓

Vocational Training Programme

Running and facilitating vocational education programs specifically for thalassemia patients, empowering them with skills for economic independence, self-reliance, and a fulfilling, productive career.

Ongoing · Empowerment
💻

Webinars & Workshops

Y.T.A regularly hosts online and in-person webinars and workshops to keep patients, caregivers, and the medical community informed about the latest in thalassemia treatment, management, and research. Topics range from transfusion protocols and chelation therapy to mental wellness, nutrition, and career development for patients. These sessions bring together leading doctors, counselors, and lived-experience speakers — making expert knowledge accessible to all, regardless of location.

Ongoing · Education & Outreach
